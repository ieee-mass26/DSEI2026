Welcome to DSEI 2026

The 1st International Workshop on Distributed and Secure Embodied Intelligence for Smart City Applications (DSEI 2026) is co-located with the 23rd IEEE International Conference on Mobile Ad-Hoc and Smart Systems (IEEE MASS 2026), and will be held on October 21–23, 2026 at The Hong Kong Polytechnic University, Hong Kong SAR, China.

The workshop will focus on frontier topics in both the theoretical and applied aspects of embodied intelligence, distributed perception and world modeling, mobile ad-hoc and smart systems, edge-native inference and split computing, and secure and trustworthy operation of embodied agents in urban environments, while particularly encouraging and supporting research that explores the intersection of these fields with smart city applications.

This includes, but is not limited to, studies on large-scale perception and world models for long-horizon navigation, communication-aware learning under time-varying latency and packet loss, collaborative multi-robot coordination and sim-to-real transfer, on-device and edge deployment of vision-language-action models, privacy-preserving embodied analytics, adversarial robustness and safety-critical control, and resilient multi-agent operation under network partitions or attacks.

It is organized by members of the Department of Computing at The Hong Kong Polytechnic University (PolyU). As a focused international workshop, it aims to provide an innovative platform for academic exchange among students, faculty members, researchers, and industry professionals from across the globe who work on distributed and secure embodied intelligence for smart cities.

Call for Papers

We invite original contributions on emerging challenges including, but not limited to, the following tracks.

Large-scale embodied perception and world models

  • Urban-scale foundation models: efficient deployment of VLA models for long-horizon navigation in non-stationary city environments.
  • Physical intelligence and multi-modal world models for anticipating dynamic traffic and pedestrian patterns.
  • Distributed spatial AI and SLAM: cooperative mapping and localization across heterogeneous agents via mobile ad-hoc networks.
  • Active sensing and perception: information-theoretic viewpoint/sensor selection to reduce uncertainty in smart cities.

Networked embodied action and coordination

  • Communication-aware reinforcement learning under time-varying latency and packet loss in MANETs.
  • Collaborative manipulation and transport: multi-robot coordination for complex urban tasks.
  • Cross-platform sim-to-real transfer from urban digital twins to physical ad-hoc robotic fleets.
  • Semantic inter-agent communication: exchanging intent and affordances instead of raw sensor data.

Edge-native systems for embodied intelligence

  • On-device inference for VLA models: hardware-aware optimization (quantization, pruning) for edge NPUs and mobile SoCs.
  • Split-computing for embodied loops: dynamic partitioning between mobile agents and smart city edge servers.
  • Energy-efficient embodied autonomy: balancing high-level reasoning with physical energy constraints.
  • Real-time reasoning at the extreme edge for micro-embodied agents.

Secure and trustworthy embodied systems (special focus)

  • Physical-world adversarial robustness (e.g., patch attacks on traffic signs, adversarial urban textures).
  • Safe-critical control in crowded spaces: verifiable RL and safety layers for human–robot interaction.
  • Privacy-preserving embodied analytics: federated learning without compromising citizen privacy.
  • Resilient embodied mesh: safety and mission integrity under partitions or attacks on ad-hoc routing.

Submission Guidelines

Authors are invited to submit original, unpublished work that is not under review elsewhere. All submissions must be written in English and prepared as PDF files using the IEEE Conference Proceedings manuscript templates, with a maximum length of six single-spaced, double-column pages in 10pt fonts on 8.5 × 11 inch paper, including all figures, tables, and references. Reviewing is single-blind, so author names may appear on the submitted manuscript. Submissions should be uploaded through EDAS; the link to the IEEE MASS 2026 workshop track will be announced on the main conference site. Accepted workshop papers are intended for inclusion in the conference proceedings and the IEEE Xplore Digital Library, subject to IEEE policies, presentation at the workshop, and IEEE’s right to exclude a paper from distribution after the conference if it is not presented.

Registration

DSEI 2026 is co-located with IEEE MASS 2026. Workshop attendees register through the main conference; please follow the registration instructions, fees, and deadlines published on the IEEE MASS 2026 website.

For each accepted workshop paper to be included in the IEEE MASS 2026 proceedings and the IEEE Xplore Digital Library, at least one author is typically required to register for the conference and present the work at the workshop, in line with IEEE MASS 2026 policies. Substitute presenters may need prior approval from the program chairs; details will be stated on the main conference site.

Registration portal and fee schedules will be linked from the IEEE MASS 2026 homepage when they become available.
